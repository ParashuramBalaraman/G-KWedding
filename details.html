Here are a few key events of a Tamil wedding

Paalikaisthaapanam

Saving Grains

The ceremony begins with the arrival of the groom accompanied by the tholan (bride’s brother). The bride's family receives the groom and garlands him to welcome him and his family. The tholan sprinkled the groom’s feet with water, a gesture lavished on an honoured guest. As a token of affection, the groom gifts the tholan with a gold ring. They both then take their place at the manavarai (wedding altar).

Kanyadhanam

Giving Away The Bride

This ceremony is significant as the priest proclaims the genealogy of the groom and bride, invoking the celestial to bear witness. The bride’s mother places the bride’s hands in the palms of the groom.
The koorai (bride sari) and thali (sacred gold pendant threaded on a gold chain) are blessed and taken around the congregation for blessing, The groom presents the koorai to the bride, who will leave to change into it.

The Thaali Ceremony

The Marriage

The bride returns wearing the koorai and garlands the groom. The solemn moment of the wedding arrives when the groom ties the thali around the bride’s neck, accompanied by a crescendo of music (to drown out any inauspicious sounds) and a showering of rice mixed with flower petals.

Sapta Padi

Walking Around The Sacred Fire

The couple take seven steps around the sacred fire in three rounds with the tholan and tholi. After the first round, the groom places the bride’s right foot on the ammi (granite stone) indicating her steadfastness in the face of life’s adversities. Displaying humility and respect for his bride, he places a metti (silver toe ring) on the right food. After the second round, he repeats this on the left foot.
